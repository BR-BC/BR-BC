Lighting-Robust Behavioral Cloning via Bayesian
Visual Encoding for Robot Manipulation with
Uncertainty-Aware Visual Representations

Anonymous

Abstract

Behavioral Cloning (BC) has demonstrated strong performance in robot manipulation by leveraging deep neural networks to learn visuomotor mappings. However, its reliability often degrades under illumination-induced distribution shifts, where vision-based policies become sensitive to overfitting and visual noise. We propose a lighting-robust BC framework that integrates Bayesian Deep Learning (BDL) into the visual encoder to enable uncertainty-aware representation learning under illumination variability. By modeling a posterior distribution over convolutional weights and optimizing the Evidence Lower Bound (ELBO), our method regularizes feature learning through a Kullback--Leibler (KL) divergence term. In simulation, our approach achieves higher success rates and shorter rollout lengths than deterministic baselines, while maintaining strong sample efficiency with a single Monte Carlo sample. In real-world experiments across seven illumination regimes, including distribution-shifted conditions, our method consistently outperforms BC baselines and maintains stable performance under severe lighting changes. These results demonstrate that Bayesian regularization induces task-aligned visual representations that improve robustness and generalization. Code and additional results are available at https://br-bc.github.io/BR-BC/.
